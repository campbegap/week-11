5-Course Korean Meal

This meal includes an appetizer, soup, salad, main course, and dessert.

Appetizer: Crispy Vegetable Spring Rolls

Fill wheat spring roll wrappers with thinly sliced cucumber, carrot, bell pepper, lettuce and rice noodles. Roll tightly and seal the edge with a sprinkle of water. Heat vegetable oil to 350 degrees fahrenheit, then fry for 3-5 minutes until golden and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels. Serve with choice of dipping sauce.

Soup: Miso Soup

Heat 12 cups of water with 3-4 tablespoons of broth until hot but not boiling. Add 2 blocks of cubed tofu, 3 tablespoons of dried wakame, and 5 sliced green onions, let it warm for a few minutes. In a bowl, whisk 3/4 cup of miso paste with hot broth until smooth, then stir into pot. Serve warm.

Salad: Cucumber Salad

Slice cucumbers thin and sprinkle with salt; let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then drain. Toss cucumbers with rice vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, chili flakes, gochugaru, and minced garlic. Top with sesame seeds and serve.

Main Course: Beef Bulgogi with Rice and Kimchi

Slice ribeye steak thinly and marinate in soy sauce, minced garlic, sugar, sesame oil and onion mixture for 30-60 minutes. Cook in a hot pan to customers liking. Serve over steamed white rice with kimchi.

Dessert: Bingsu

Shave ice until fluffy and put into a bowl. Drizzle with sweetened condensed milk, then top with mango, strawberries, mochi pieces, sweet red bean and more condensed milk. Serve immediately.
